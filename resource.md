## 1. National FLN Programs & Frameworks

### 1.1 NIPUN Bharat

**NIPUN Bharat** — the National Initiative for Proficiency in Reading with Understanding and Numeracy — was launched on 5th July 2021 by the Department of School Education and Literacy under the Ministry of Education. Its vision is to create an enabling environment to ensure the universal acquisition of foundational literacy and numeracy, so that every child achieves the desired learning competencies in reading, writing, and numeracy by the end of Grade 3, by 2026–27.

The NIPUN Bharat initiative focuses on several primary components:
- Establishing FLN targets specific to each state
- Enhancing teacher training
- Ensuring school readiness
- Implementing digital tracking systems

Implemented under the Samagra Shiksha scheme (umbrella programme for school education):
- Budget for Samagra Shiksha increased by 20% from Rs. 31,050 crores (2021-22) to Rs. 37,383 crores
- NCERT is responsible for developing learning materials, defining outcomes, and building teacher capacity
- Every Anganwadi Centre and foundational-grade school must follow the NIPUN framework
- Emphasizes mother tongue / home language as the medium of instruction during foundational years
- Promotes activity-based, discovery, and play-based teaching methods
- Each state is required to formulate its own FLN action plan

### 1.2 National Education Policy (NEP) 2020

NEP 2020 is the driving policy document behind FLN reforms in India. Key provisions directly relevant to FLN include:
- Strong emphasis on Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE) as a critical foundation
- Home/local language mandated as medium of instruction at least until Grade 5, preferably until Grade 8
- Three-language formula made flexible — no language can be imposed on any state
- Foundational stage (age 3-8) treated as a distinct curricular phase requiring specialized pedagogy
- 25% of primary school children face a moderate to severe learning disadvantage because classroom language differs from home language (World Bank, 2021)

### 1.3 Vidya Pravesh

A 3-month school readiness program developed by NCERT for children entering Grade 1. That includes play-based learning, pre-literacy, pre-numeracy, and communication activities.

### 1.4 NISHTHA FLN

NISHTHA FLN is a teacher training programme designed to help teachers understand:
- How children learn in early grades
- How to teach reading and numeracy effectively
- Activity-based learning methods
- Child psychology
- Assessment techniques
- Inclusive classrooms
- Competency-based education

### 1.5 DIKSHA

DIKSHA platform provides all these features for students from Classes 1 to 12. It is a national digital learning platform launched by NCERT and the Ministry of Education, Government of India. Some important features:
- Audio learning content
- Educational videos
- E-textbooks and PDFs
- Interactive quizzes and assessments
- Practice questions and worksheets
- QR-code based textbook learning
- Multilingual support
- Teacher training courses
- Offline content access

### 1.6 PARAKH

PARAKH evaluates students based on understanding, problem-solving, reading, numeracy, critical thinking, and real-life application of learning instead of only memorization. It also focuses on holistic development, including communication, creativity, and participation.

Class 3 PARAKH test pdf

### 1.7 Jadui Pitara

Jaadui Pitara is an NCERT play-based learning kit for children aged 3–8 years. It includes toys, games, storybooks, and activities to make learning joyful, interactive, and skill-based according to NEP 2020's Foundational Stage.

## 2. Global Benchmarks & Standards

### 2.1 GPF (Global Proficiency Framework)

The Global Proficiency Framework defines minimum proficiency levels (MPLs) for SDG 4.1.1 reporting.

### 2.2 TIMSS

**TIMSS** is the world's largest and longest-running international assessment of Mathematics and Science achievement.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Full name | Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study |
| Run by | IEA (International Association for the Evaluation of Educational Achievement) |
| First held | 1995 |
| Frequency | Every 4 years (1995, 1999, 2003, 2007, 2011, 2015, 2019, 2023) |
| Grades | Grade 4 (age ~10) and Grade 8 (age ~14) |
| Subjects | Mathematics + Science |
| Countries | 64 countries + 6 benchmarking systems (TIMSS 2023) |
| Students | 650,000+ students assessed globally (TIMSS 2023) |
| Format | Digital assessment (since 2019); fully digital 2023 |
| SDG link | Reports on SDG 4.1.1 (b) and (c) via GPF linking |

**TIMSS Benchmarks:**

| Benchmark | Score | What students can do |
|-----------|-------|---------------------|
| ADVANCED | 625 | Apply understanding in complex/unfamiliar situations; reason, generalise, solve multi-step problems |
| HIGH | 550 | Apply mathematical knowledge and understanding to solve problems in a variety of situations |
| INTERMEDIATE | 475 | Apply basic mathematical knowledge in straightforward situations; demonstrate understanding of whole numbers, fractions |
| LOW | 400 | Basic mathematical knowledge such as adding and subtracting up to 3-digit numbers and applying basic properties of geometric shapes |

> **Note:** 'Low' (400) is the minimum baseline, NOT the MPL. The GPF 'Meets MPL' (Level 3) roughly corresponds to TIMSS Intermediate (475) benchmark.

### 2.3 SDG 4 — Quality Education

United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 4 *"Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all"* by 2030.

SDG 4 is one of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals adopted in 2015 by all 193 UN member countries (including India) as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development.

- India signed the 2030 Agenda and is committed to all SDG 4 targets
- Key programs aligned to SDG 4: NIPUN Bharat Mission, National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020), Samagra Shiksha, PARAKH
- Current gaps:
  - 56% of children at end of primary are Learning Poor (reading)
  - India has NOT reported SDG 4.1.1 data in the last 4 years
  - National assessments not yet formally linked to GPF scale

### 2.6 World Bank — FLN Initiatives in India

**Core Pedagogical & Structural Strategies:**

- **Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL):** Groups children by current learning level rather than grade. Originally developed by Pratham; now widely used for early grade remediation.
- **NIPUN Bharat:** Mandates a shift to joyful, play-based pedagogy (storytelling, games, puzzles, puppets). Includes "Vidya Pravesh".
- **Multilingual Instruction:** NEP 2020 emphasizes teaching in the child's home language (e.g., Odisha's tribal MTB-MLE program, J&K's Urdu/Punjabi initiatives).
- **Digital Learning & EdTech:** DIKSHA platform hosts multilingual content. State-specific apps: UP (Gunvatta App & Prerna Portal), Telangana (Telangana Education App), Bihar (Tangerine).
- **Structured Pedagogy:** Prescribes dedicated, protected FLN time blocks (e.g., UP mandates 120 minutes daily).
- **Community Engagement:** Tamil Nadu (Illam Thedi Kalvi), Karnataka (FLN Melas).
- **Nutrition Integration:** "Poshan Bhi, Padhai Bhi" (2023–24) reorients Anganwadis to combine nutrition services with structured early learning.
- **Teacher Training:** NISHTHA FLN (12-module program on DIKSHA). States use monthly learning circles and e-magazines (e.g., Bihar's NIPUN Samvad).
- **Formative Assessment:** Replaces annual exams with low-stakes, frequent checks (e.g., UP's thrice-yearly Prerna assessments, Telangana's baseline-midline-endline tracking).

### 3.4 Key Findings for Research Project

1. **Strong alignment at Grades 1–3 (NIPUN mission grades):** NIPUN Bharat's Grade 2 and Grade 3 Lakshyas directly mirror the GPF Minimum Proficiency Level (MPL) targets in both literacy (60 wpm + comprehension) and numeracy (3-digit operations).

2. **Partial alignment at Grade 4:** India's NAS covers similar skill domains as GPF at Grade 4, but without formal policy linking (cut-score mapping), results cannot be placed on the global 4-level proficiency scale.

3. **Critical data gap at Grade 5 (SDG 4.1.1 reporting point):** India's last NAS Grade 5 data is from 2017 and is not GPF-linked. India is listed as 'data unavailable' in the UNESCO-UIS global database for over 4 years.

4. **Your research fills the most critical gap:** A national FLN assessment at Grade 3 and Grade 5, aligned to GPF, would directly update India's Learning Poverty figure (currently 56% based on 2017 data) and restore India's visibility in SDG 4.1.1 global reporting.

5. **India's frameworks are conceptually strong:** NEP 2020, NIPUN Bharat, and PARAKH demonstrate India's policy intent to align with global FLN standards. The gap is in formal assessment linkage and current data.

## 4. Assessment Frameworks

### 4.1 NAS (National Achievement Survey)

Conducted by PARAKH (Performance Assessment, Review, and Analysis of Knowledge for Holistic Development), which operates under the NCERT and the Ministry of Education, Government of India.
- Large-scale assessment of students
- Covers Classes 3, 5, 8, and 10
- Measures learning outcomes in Language, Mathematics, Science, etc.
- Provides state-wise and national learning data.

### 4.2 FLS (Foundational Learning Study)

- Conducted by NCERT in 2022
- Focuses specifically on Class 3 FLN skills
- Assesses reading fluency, reading comprehension, and numeracy
- Conducted in about 20 languages with around 86,000 students
- Created to establish FLN benchmarks under NIPUN Bharat

The study targets Grade 3 students across 10,000+ schools in all 36 States/UTs. Up to 12 students per school are selected through a systematic random sampling process — using the school's PIN code to generate a random start, then selecting every nth student from the class register.

**How the Assessment is Conducted:** The assessment is one-on-one, oral, and tablet-based. A trained Field Investigator (FI) sits with each child individually and administers the test using a digital app (developed by IIT Delhi). The child responds verbally or by pointing/touching the screen — they are never coached, prompted, or penalized.

### 4.3 ASER (Annual Status of Education Report)

ASER is Pratham/ASER Centre's annual rural household survey. It assesses basic reading and arithmetic abilities of children aged 5–16, and for the first time in 2024, also measured digital literacy among 14–16-year-olds. ASER 2024 reached 649,491 children across 17,997 villages in 605 rural districts. ASER popularized the "Teaching at the Right Level" approach.

### 4.4 EAC-PM FLN Report

- Published under the guidance of the Economic Advisory Council to the Prime Minister (EAC-PM)
- Does not conduct assessments itself
- Uses data from NAS and FLS
- Creates the FLN Index and state rankings
- Analyzes why some states perform better or worse in foundational learning

The Index on Foundational Learning (EAC-PM) is a composite benchmarking tool covering 36 indicators across five domains: Educational Infrastructure, Access to Education, Basic Health, Learning Outcomes, and Governance, built using Principal Component Analysis and used to rank states/UTs against each other.

### 4.5 Assessment Framework Comparison

| Assessment | Conducted by | Scope | Grade/Age Focus |
|------------|-------------|-------|-----------------|
| ASER (Annual Status of Education Report) | Pratham (NGO) | Rural household survey, all districts | Age 3-16, all grades |
| NAS (National Achievement Survey) | NCERT / Ministry of Education | School-based, census sample | Grade 3, 5, 8, 10 |
| FLS (Foundational Learning Study) 2022 | MoE + NCERT + UNICEF | School-based, 20 languages | Grade 3 students only |

### 4.6 India's International Assessment Gap

India does not participate in any international large-scale assessment (like PIRLS or PASEC) that is officially linked to the GPF. India uses its own National Learning Assessment (NLA), which is run by PARAKH under the Ministry of Education. Because India's test is not formally mapped to the GPF, its data has limited global comparability — the World Bank uses it as a best-available proxy but notes this as a significant data gap.

### 4.7 Why FLN Matters

FLN — basic reading with comprehension and basic arithmetic — is treated as the precondition for all later learning. NEP 2020 is explicit about this: attaining foundational literacy and numeracy for all children is described as an urgent national mission.

The EAC-PM report frames the stakes in human and economic terms. When children cannot read, write, and understand simple text, they struggle to learn anything else in school, making them more likely to repeat grades and drop out — outcomes that cascade into poor health, high youth unemployment, and entrenched poverty.

The pandemic sharpened the urgency. In low- and middle-income countries, the share of 10-year-olds unable to read and understand simple text jumped from 57% to 70% between 2019 and 2022, and India alone saw roughly 250 million students affected by school closures, with 92% of Indian students losing at least one language ability and 82% losing at least one math ability (UNICEF, 2022).

### 4.8 How India Measures FLN

Three main instruments are used:
1. **National Achievement Survey (NAS)** — run periodically since 2001, testing Language, Mathematics, and Environmental Studies at Grades 3, 5, 8, and 10.
2. **Foundational Learning Study (FLS) 2022** — more FLN-specific, tested over 86,000 Grade 3 students in 20 languages using GPF.
3. **ASER (Annual Status of Education Report)** — Pratham/ASER Centre's annual rural household survey.

### 5.6 Key Findings & Challenges

- **Literacy-numeracy gap:** Only 30.7% of Class 5 students can solve a basic division problem vs. 48.7% who read fluently — an 18 percentage point gap (ASER 2024). No state reports higher numeracy than literacy.
- **Top performers:** UP's Mission Prerna (launched 2019) demonstrates how early, mission-mode reform with top-down accountability and real-time monitoring delivers results.
- **Unexpected underperformers:** Many southern states have not matched the gains seen in UP, Bihar, and Himachal Pradesh.
- **Marginalised groups:** Grade 3 students from pucca houses are roughly twice as likely to read at grade level vs. those in katcha homes. Girls whose mothers attended school show 2.2–2.4x higher literacy levels.
- **NIPUN Bharat target:** Universal FLN by Grade 3 by 2026–27. Only 1 in 4 Grade 3 students reads at grade level in government schools.

## 7. Child Psychology & Pedagogy

### 7.1 Foundational Numeracy Definition

Foundational Numeracy means the ability to reason and to apply simple numerical concepts in daily life problem solving. It includes the ability to:
- Make an understanding of quantities
- Develop concepts like more and less, and larger and smaller
- Establish relationships between single items and groups of items
- Use symbols that represent quantities (7 means the same thing as seven)
- Compare numbers (10 is greater than 8, and three is half of six)
- Arrange numbers in a list in order: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.
- Visualise shapes and space around them

Children grow up counting their toys, toffees, people at home or other small sets of objects. When schools begin to develop the understanding of pre-number concept, they should build upon the child's experience from his/her familiar context.

**Classification:** Putting together things that have some characteristics in common. Start with one property, gradually increase complexity.

**Seriation:** Ordering a set of objects according to some rule. Forms the base for understanding of patterns.

**One-to-one correspondence:** Matching or pairing of objects. Children need to understand 'many and few', 'more than/less than' and 'as many as'.

### 7.2 NISHTHA FLN — Child Psychology Points

#### 7.2.1 Brain Development & Neuroscience
- Over 85% of a child's cumulative brain development occurs before age 6
- The brain is most flexible and adaptable to learning during early childhood
- Positive early experiences support lifelong learning and development

### 7.3 Cognitive Load Analysis

Cognitive load refers to the amount of mental effort required to solve a question. Children have limited working memory, so questions requiring more information processing become difficult even if the concept is known.

A child may fail a question not because they don't know the concept, but because:
- Working memory gets overloaded
- Too many visual elements are present
- Multiple steps are required
- The question format is unfamiliar

### 7.6 Why Comparison as the Foundation of Numeracy

Jean Piaget, in *The Child's Conception of Number* (1941), showed that children's relationship with numbers starts with one simple skill: comparison. Before a child can count, order, or conserve quantity, they can already tell which pile has more. Every higher numerical skill builds on this basic ability to compare.

## 8. Technical & Project Notes

### 8.1 Technical Note: Computer Vision vs Standard OCR/ICR

The Core Problem: Traditional OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is engineered strictly for machine-printed text, and standard ICR (Intelligent Character Recognition) is limited to recognizing isolated handwritten characters inside structured boxes.

The Challenge: To keep the assessment highly engaging for young learners, our worksheets preserve original, creative, freehand child actions — such as drawing matching lines, tracing paths, and looping scattered clusters. Standard OCR and ICR fail completely here because they cannot interpret multi-directional pencil lines, overlapping loops, or spatial drawings.

The Solution: Rather than forcing children into restrictive text boxes, this project uses an Advanced Computer Vision (OpenCV) and Spatial Coordinate Mapping pipeline. The backend detects the exact (X, Y) coordinates of a child's hand-drawn marks and matches those paths directly against our Master Answer Key JSON coordinates. This preserves 100% of the creative formatting while achieving automated grading accuracy.

### Section 1: Background and Policy Framework

#### 1.1 Background and Policy Context

Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN) refers to the ability of a child to read with comprehension and perform basic arithmetic operations by the time they complete Grade 3 (approximately age 8–9). It constitutes the bedrock of all future learning.

According to the World Bank, approximately 50% of children in India lack foundational learning. By Grade 5, these gaps become entrenched and extremely difficult to bridge.

#### 1.2 The NIPUN Bharat Mission

The Government of India launched NIPUN Bharat on 5th July 2021. The mission envisions that every child in India will achieve desired foundational literacy and numeracy competencies by the end of Grade 3 — covering children aged 3 to 9 years — by 2026–27.

- Implemented under the Samagra Shiksha scheme
- Budget for Samagra Shiksha increased by 20% from Rs. 31,050 crores (2021-22) to Rs. 37,383 crores
- NCERT responsible for developing learning materials, defining outcomes, and building teacher capacity
- Every Anganwadi Centre and foundational-grade school must follow the NIPUN framework
- Emphasizes mother tongue / home language as the medium of instruction
- Promotes activity-based, discovery, and play-based teaching methods
- Each state is required to formulate its own FLN action plan

#### 1.3 National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 and FLN

- Strong emphasis on Early Childhood Care and Education (ECCE)
- Home/local language mandated as medium of instruction at least until Grade 5
- Three-language formula made flexible
- Foundational stage (age 3–8) treated as a distinct curricular phase
- 25% of primary school children face moderate to severe learning disadvantage due to language mismatch (World Bank, 2021)
