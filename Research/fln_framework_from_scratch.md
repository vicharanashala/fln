# FLN Numeracy Framework — Built From Scratch (Research-Grounded)

**Status: DRAFT working document. Not yet compared against the repo's existing 59 levels. Not committed to the repo.** This document went through three rounds of external review before reaching this version — see the **Appendix: How This Document Was Built** at the end for the full revision history: what was wrong in earlier drafts, how it was caught, and what changed. The body below states only the final, current position.

**Method:** Concept decomposition (Topic → Concept → Sub-concept/cognitive operation → common misconception), per the Learning Trajectories approach (Clements & Sarama, 2009/2021) and Design Principles P1–P9 in `numeracy_research_synthesis` (Pavani, 2026). Every stage cites its governing source — no uncited claims. Level *count* is not fixed in advance; concepts are sequenced by cognitive dependency (ALEKS/Knowledge Space Theory — prerequisite graph, not fixed age-per-level), consistent with SRS.md §1.4's own principle that levels are milestones, not gates.

**Scope:** Preschool 1 (age 3–4) → Class 4 (age 9–10), Mathematics only, matching the platform's own scope (PRD.md, SRS.md).

**Strands** (per SRS.md §12.1 topic taxonomy + `numeracy_research_synthesis` §1.2 NIPUN Bharat domains — the two independently agree): Pre-Number Foundations, Number Sense, Number Operations, Shapes & Spatial Understanding, Measurement, Patterns, Money, Calendar & Time, Fractions, Data Handling. Not every strand is active at every stage — a strand enters the ladder only when research grounds its introduction at that stage (see per-stage tables).

## Design Principles Governing This Framework (all cited, none invented)

| # | Principle | Source |
|---|---|---|
| DP1 | Comparison precedes *cardinal* (meaningful) counting, ordering, and conservation — sequence comparison-type concepts first within any stage. Rote verbal counting (reciting the count sequence with no object-tracking) is a memorized sequence, not counting in Piaget's sense, and is exempt from this ordering. | Piaget, *La genèse du nombre chez l'enfant* (1941) / English translation *The Child's Conception of Number* (1952) — one source, not two independent studies (`Child_psychology.md` §9; `numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1) |
| DP2 | Concrete → Representational → Abstract (CRA): every concept introduced with physical objects, then pictures/diagrams, then symbols. Skipping to abstract too early produces procedural mimicry without understanding. | `numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1, §9.1 (P2) |
| DP3 | One new cognitive demand per concept at introduction; demands combine only once each is separately fluent. | Sweller, 1988, Cognitive Load Theory (`Child_psychology.md` §8; `numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.3, §9.1 P4) |
| DP4 | Topic ≠ Concept ≠ Sub-concept. A topic (e.g. "Place Value") contains multiple concepts; naming the topic in a worksheet doesn't mean the concept is tested. | Battista, 2012; Fuson, 1990 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §5.1, §9.2 P6) |
| DP5 | Concepts are tagged conceptual, procedural, or integrated — not assumed to transfer from one to the other. | Rittle-Johnson & Alibali, 1999; Rittle-Johnson, Siegler & Alibali, 2001 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §7, §9.2 P9) |
| DP6 | Difficulty (empirical, % who get it right) ≠ cognitive progression (epistemological, hierarchy of understanding). Sequencing must follow the latter. | `numeracy_research_synthesis` §6.1 |
| DP7 | Every concept assessed across ≥2 representations (object count, finger count, abstract numeral, word problem) — single-format competence is not mastery. | Siegler & Booth, 2004; Dehaene, 2011 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §9.1 P5) |
| DP8 | 50/35/15 easy/moderate/hard question-mix per assessment, to avoid triggering guessing or math anxiety. **Labeled explicitly as a platform convention, not an established research standard:** `Child_psychology.md` §8.2 states this ratio with no primary-source citation of its own (unlike Sweller, Fuson, or Rittle-Johnson elsewhere in this table) — checked directly, the file gives no reference for it. Presenting it with the same citation weight as the peer-reviewed principles around it would borrow authority it hasn't earned; it's followed here because it's a reasonable design default, not because it's been externally validated. | `Child_psychology.md` §8.2 (platform convention — no traceable primary source) |
| DP9 | Levels are milestones a child is progressed toward, not sequential gates each must be individually cleared. | SRS.md §1.4 (platform's own spec) |
| DP10 | Assessment-as-Learning: items should reveal thinking (ask "why"/"is X right?"), not just yield a right/wrong mark, and should target known misconceptions directly. | Earl, 2003/2006 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §9.3) |

**Caveat on DP1, stated honestly rather than presented as settled:** Piaget's stage account is not the only credible developmental model here, and it sits in tension with this framework's *other* main pillar. Post-Piagetian work — Gelman & Gallistel's counting principles (1978) and the approximate-number-system literature — found genuine counting and quantity-discrimination competence earlier than Piaget's preoperational-stage account predicts. Clements & Sarama, whose Learning Trajectories this framework leans on for the counting chain (Stage 1–3) and the DP2/DP3 CRA principles, explicitly built their trajectories partly *in opposition to* strict Piagetian readiness constraints — they argue instructional quality can move a child well ahead of the "expected" Piagetian age. DP1 is retained here as a practical sequencing heuristic (comparison-type items before abstract-counting items reads as good design advice regardless of which developmental theory is "correct"), not as an uncontested theoretical claim. Leaning on both Piaget and Clements & Sarama without flagging this tension would be the kind of uncited-confidence problem this framework is trying not to repeat from the repo's level files.

## A note on Preschool 1/2/3 before building them

NIPUN Bharat's Lakshya Soochi specifies only **one** preschool target — "Balvatika / Age 5–6" (`FLN_foundation.md` §4) — the *exit* competency of the entire 3-year preschool block, not three separate yearly targets. The Foundational Stage's 5-year structure (3 preschool years + Grades 1–2, ages 3–8) is defined by **NCF-FS 2022** (National Curriculum Framework for the Foundational Stage, published 20 October 2022 — the dedicated document for this age band); NCF-SE 2023 (National Curriculum Framework for School Education, the broader K-12 document) incorporates and restates the same 5+3+3+4 structure a year later (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §1.3 cites the 2023 document specifically), but the Foundational Stage's own detailed content — including the subitizing emphasis behind Stage 1/2's nodes below — lives in the 2022 document. Neither document itemizes the 3 preschool years year-by-year for numeracy. So Preschool 1 (age 3–4) and Preschool 2 (age 4–5) below are **not independently government-specified** — they are backward-constructed from the Balvatika age-5–6 endpoint using Clements & Sarama's empirically-derived counting trajectory and Piaget's preoperational-stage research, working out what must be true at ages 3–4 and 4–5 for a child to plausibly reach the age-5–6 target. This is stated explicitly because it is a real methodological difference from Class 1–4 below, where every stage has a directly-specified government target. **Flag for comparison:** the existing repo also splits Preschool into 3 stages without citing a source for the split — worth checking whether it did the same backward-construction or invented the split independently.

## Stage 1 — Preschool 1 (Age 3–4)

**Piaget's stage:** Early preoperational (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1) — symbolic thought emerging; logical operations (classification, seriation, conservation) not yet reliable.
**Counting trajectory position:** Reciter → Corresponder (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2, Clements & Sarama) — verbally counts to ~10 by rote; begins genuine one-to-one correspondence (each count-word to exactly one object), but not yet accurate cardinality on larger sets. **Calibration note:** placing "Reciter (10)" at age 3–4 is the optimistic end of Clements & Sarama's own range, which places it nearer age 4 — acceptable as a stage *target* rather than an average, and consistent with the wide real-world variance this framework already accepts for older stages (e.g. Stage 6's ASER-documented below-level reality).
**Pre-number building blocks active:** Classification (single property only — colour *or* shape, not both) and one-to-one correspondence begin here; seriation is not yet expected (`Child_psychology.md` §2; `FLN_foundation.md` §3).

| Strand | Concept | Cognitive operation / sub-concept | CRA stage | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-Number Foundations | One-to-one correspondence | Matching one object to one object (not yet one object to one number-word reliably) | Concrete only | NIPUN Bharat §3 (`FLN_foundation.md`); Clements & Sarama "Corresponder" (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2) |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Classification (single property) | Groups objects by *one* observable attribute (colour, size, or shape — not combined) | Concrete only | `FLN_foundation.md` §3; `Child_psychology.md` §2 |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Perceptual same/different | Visually judges whether two small sets "look the same" or "look different" — a *perceptual* judgement, not yet a *quantity* judgement (this precedes true more/less comparison, which is a Stage 2 concept below) | Concrete only | Piaget preoperational-stage distinction (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1) — DP1 applies to quantity comparison specifically, which is not yet reliable at this age |
| Number Sense | Rote verbal counting to 10 | Recites the count sequence 1–10 without necessarily tracking objects | Concrete/oral | Clements & Sarama "Reciter" (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2) |
| Number Sense | Counting small sets (1–3) with support | Touches/points while counting 1–3 real objects | Concrete only | Clements & Sarama trajectory; `Child_psychology.md` §5 (kinaesthetic preference) |
| Shapes & Spatial | Shape matching (perceptual) | Matches identical real-world shapes by sight, not yet by named properties | Concrete only | `Child_psychology.md` §2 (perception-bound thinking precedes concept-based understanding); Van Hiele Level 0 "Visualization" *(citation added 2026-07-19, see appendix)* — holistic, appearance-based shape judgement, the documented prerequisite to property-based reasoning at Stage 3 (S3.9) |
| Number Sense | Perceptual subitizing | Instantly recognises the quantity of 1–3 objects *without counting* — a distinct trajectory from counting, not a byproduct of it | Concrete only | Clements & Sarama's subitizing trajectory, perceptual subitizing ~ages 1–4 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2 domain) |
| Patterns | *Not yet introduced* | Pattern recognition requires holding a repeating unit in working memory — a Stage 2+ skill per the difficulty ladder | — | `Child_psychology.md` §8.1 (easiest pattern tier is still 2-item, assumes some working-memory capacity) |

**Design note (DP3, DP2):** every concept here is concrete-only — no representational or abstract items belong at this stage. This is the strand where over-reach is most likely (Class Level 1's stated objective vs. its actual worksheet, flagged in the memory review, will need re-checking against *this* table specifically once comparison begins).

## Stage 2 — Preschool 2 (Age 4–5)

**Piaget's stage:** Mid preoperational — Piaget's core finding (comparison precedes counting/ordering/conservation) becomes actionable here: children can now reliably judge which of two *groups* has more/less, even before they can count either group exactly (`Child_psychology.md` §9; DP1).
**Counting trajectory position:** Corresponder → Counter of Small Numbers — counts up to 5 objects accurately *with cardinality* (knows the last number said = the total) (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2).
**Pre-number building blocks active:** Seriation begins (3 objects, one rule — e.g. tallest to shortest); classification extends to increasing complexity within a single property (`FLN_foundation.md` §3).

| Strand | Concept | Cognitive operation / sub-concept | CRA stage | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-Number Foundations | Quantity comparison — more/less/equal | True group-quantity judgement (not perceptual matching): visually compares two groups of objects and judges more/less/equal *without necessarily counting* | Concrete only | Piaget, 1941 — comparison-before-counting (`Child_psychology.md` §9); this is the correctly-grounded home of "Quantity Comparison" as a *concept*, distinct from Stage 1's perceptual same/different |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Seriation (3 objects) | Orders 3 objects by one rule (biggest→smallest); does not yet reliably grasp transitivity (A>B, B>C ⟹ A>C) | Concrete only | `FLN_foundation.md` §3 |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Classification (single property, increasing set size) | Sorts larger/more varied sets by one attribute | Concrete only | `FLN_foundation.md` §3 |
| Number Sense | Counting to 5 with cardinality | Counts a set of up to 5 objects and states the total accurately | Concrete only | Clements & Sarama "Counter of Small Numbers" (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2) |
| Number Sense | Counting 6–10 with support | Extends counting range with scaffolding; not yet independent | Concrete only | Same trajectory, approaching "Counter/Producer to 10+" |
| Shapes & Spatial | Shape identification (named) | Names common 2D shapes (circle, square, triangle) by sight | Concrete/pictorial | `Child_psychology.md` §5 (visual-format preference) |
| Shapes & Spatial | Shape composition — piece assembly *(added 2026-07-19, see appendix)* | Combines shapes (e.g. pattern blocks) via trial and error, treating pieces as separate parts of a picture rather than a planned whole — a distinct skill from shape *identification* above: building with shapes, not naming them | Concrete only | Clements, Sarama, Baroody, Joswick & Wolfe (2019) shape-composition learning trajectory — "Piece Assembler" level |
| Patterns | Simple repeating pattern (2-item AB cycle) | Extends 🔴🔵🔴🔵🔴 __ patterns; 2-item cycle only | Concrete/pictorial | `Child_psychology.md` §8.1 (explicit easy-tier example) |
| Measurement | Comparative vocabulary (informal) | Uses longer/shorter, heavier/lighter, bigger/smaller in concrete comparisons (not yet units) | Concrete only | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 (Balvatika target lists this explicitly, backward-mapped as beginning here) |
| Number Sense | Conceptual subitizing | Instantly sees a slightly larger set as composed of smaller recognized parts (e.g. sees 5 dots as "3 and 2" without counting each one) — the bridge between perceptual subitizing (Stage 1) and true counting/cardinality | Concrete only | Clements & Sarama's subitizing trajectory, conceptual subitizing ~ages 4–5 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2 domain) — matches this stage's age range exactly |

## Stage 3 — Preschool 3 / Balvatika (Age 5–6)

**This is the only preschool stage with a directly government-specified target** — NIPUN Bharat's Balvatika Lakshya, verbatim: *"Counts & correlates numerals to 10; recognizes/reads numerals to 10; compares two groups (more/less/equal); arranges by sequence; classifies objects by observable characteristics; uses comparative vocabulary"* (`FLN_foundation.md` §4).
**Counting trajectory position:** Counter and Producer to 10+ — counts *and produces* (on request, "give me 7") sets beyond 10 (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §4.2).
**Piaget's stage:** Late preoperational, approaching the concrete-operational transition — seriation with transitivity and multi-property classification become possible for the first time (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1).

| Strand | Concept | Cognitive operation / sub-concept | CRA stage | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Number Sense | Numeral recognition (1–10) | Reads/identifies written numerals 1–10 (symbol recognition, distinct from — but built on — counting fluency) | Pictorial/abstract (symbol) | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 (Lakshya, verbatim); counting fluency genuinely supports learning to recognise the numerals for those counts, consistent with the companion networks document's S2.5→S3.1 edge |
| Number Sense | Numeral–quantity correspondence | Correlates a numeral (e.g. "7") with a counted group of 7 objects — links Stage 1–2's counting work to the Stage 3 symbol | Concrete→pictorial→abstract (full CRA arc, per DP2) | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 ("counts & correlates numerals to 10") |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Numeral comparison (object-mediated) | Compares two *numerals* by first re-grounding each in a counted/object representation (e.g. "7 vs 3" via counting objects for each) — **abstract numeral-vs-numeral comparison with no object mediation is NOT yet a Balvatika target**; that is a Class 1 concept below | Concrete/pictorial (object-mediated); NOT yet abstract | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 ("compares two groups more/less/equal" — groups, not bare numerals); DP6 (cognitive progression, not just difficulty) |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Seriation with transitivity | Orders objects by a rule and can infer A>C from A>B, B>C | Concrete only | `numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1; `FLN_foundation.md` §3 |
| Pre-Number Foundations | Classification (observable characteristics, flexible) | Classifies by any observable characteristic, can re-sort the same set by a different attribute | Concrete/pictorial | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 (Lakshya, verbatim) |
| Number Sense | Sequencing (arranges by sequence) | Arranges numbers/objects/events in order | Concrete/pictorial | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 (Lakshya, verbatim) — this is the direct precursor to Class 1's "ordering" concept |
| Measurement | Comparative vocabulary (formalizing) | Uses longer/shorter, heavier/lighter correctly and consistently, extending Stage 2 | Concrete/pictorial | `FLN_foundation.md` §4 (Lakshya, verbatim) |
| Patterns | 2-item pattern independent + intro to 3-item | Extends 2-item cycles independently; 3-item cycles (🔴🔵🟢 repeat) introduced with support | Concrete/pictorial | `Child_psychology.md` §8.1 (hard tier = 3-item cycle, appropriately later than Stage 2's 2-item) |
| Shapes & Spatial | Shape properties (basic) | Begins describing *why* a shape is a triangle (3 sides) rather than only naming it | Pictorial | Bridges toward NCERT Grade 1's "describes 3D shape properties" (`FLN_foundation.md` §4, Class I row); Van Hiele Level 1 "Analysis" *(citation added 2026-07-19, see appendix)* — property-based reasoning, sequentially dependent on Stage 1's Level-0 holistic shape judgement (S1.6) |
| Shapes & Spatial | Shape composition — picture making *(added 2026-07-19, see appendix)* | Lays shapes side-by-side with trial and error, beginning to recognize and plan combinations of shapes into a picture — extends Stage 2's piece-assembly into planned (not just accidental) combination | Concrete/pictorial | Clements, Sarama, Baroody, Joswick & Wolfe (2019) shape-composition learning trajectory — "Picture Maker" level |

**Design note (DP6):** the numeral-comparison row above is the single most important cell for this framework's later comparison against the repo — it directly addresses last session's Level 10 finding (repo's Level 10, "Comparison – Numeral," Preschool 3/age 5–6). NIPUN Bharat's own Balvatika Lakshya says "compares two **groups**," not bare numerals — object-mediated numeral comparison is the Balvatika-appropriate target; fully abstract numeral-vs-numeral comparison (e.g. "8 vs 3" with no object grounding) is a Class 1 concept in the next stage, not Preschool 3.

## Stage 4 — Class 1 (Age 6–7)

**Government target, verbatim:** *"Counts objects to 20; reads/writes numbers to 99; adds & subtracts to 9 in daily contexts; describes 3D shape properties (corners, edges, surfaces); estimates length (non-standard units — hand span, footstep) and capacity (cup, spoon, mug); creates/recites poems using shapes & numbers"* (`FLN_foundation.md` §4, Class I row). NIPUN Bharat's shorter Lakshya framing agrees: "Reads and writes numbers up to 99; performs simple addition and subtraction" (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §1.2).
**Piaget's stage:** Entering concrete operational — can now reason logically about concrete objects, still needs physical/representational anchors for anything beyond (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §3.1).
**Assessment-design flag:** the "poems using shapes & numbers" target is explicitly **not paper-testable** — `Assessment_paper_rubric.md`'s Class 1 blueprint tags it "Create (best assessed via oral/classroom task, not written paper)" and weights it 10% but flags it non-paper-based. A worksheet-only platform structurally cannot assess this Lakshya item; that's a scope gap to be explicit about, not silently dropped.

## A note on which document governs Class 2 onward

From this stage on, two different kinds of source disagree in ways worth naming rather than silently resolving (matching the caution the Class 2 competitor-progression doc itself models — see `Class_2_Adaptive_Question_Progression...md` §2, "differences are preserved as observed rather than normalized"):

- **NIPUN Bharat's Lakshya Soochi** (`FLN_foundation.md`) is a *statutory minimum* — the floor every child must reach. `numeracy_research_synthesis` §1.4 states this explicitly: NCERT Learning Outcomes "establish the minimum competency bar, not the ceiling."
- **NCERT's actual textbook scope** (Math Magic series, synthesized in `numeracy_research_synthesis` §2's topic-by-topic map) generally exceeds the Lakshya floor — this is by design, not an error.
- **`Assessment_paper_rubric.md`'s per-class LO tables are Baseline-test blueprints, not end-of-year curriculum targets** — per SRS.md §1.4, "Baseline covers the complete syllabus of the *previous* class." Its "Class 3: Baseline Test" table therefore describes **Class 2's** target, not Class 3's — and it disagrees with `FLN_foundation.md` on a hard number: the Baseline table says Class 2 numbers go "up to 99," while the Lakshya table says "up to 999." **This is a direct contradiction inside the existing research corpus itself**, not something this framework introduces — flagged here rather than silently picking a side. This framework uses NCERT's textbook scope (999, matching the Lakshya table) as the primary target below, since it's corroborated by two independent sources against the Baseline table's one; but this should be checked against the actual NCERT Class 2 Math Magic textbook before being treated as settled.

## Stage 5 — Class 2 (Age 7–8)

**Government target, verbatim:** *"Reads/writes numbers to 999; adds & subtracts to 99; performs multiplication as repeated addition, division as equal sharing; builds tables of 2, 3, 4; estimates length/capacity with non-standard uniform units (rod, thread, cup); identifies 2D shapes (rectangle, triangle, circle, oval); uses spatial vocabulary (far/near, above/below, front/behind); creates/solves simple riddles with numbers & shapes"* (`FLN_foundation.md` §4, Class II row).
**NCERT textbook scope (fuller than the Lakshya floor):** 3-digit addition/subtraction with carrying/borrowing; multiplication introduced via equal groups; division via equal sharing (pictorial); informal fractions ("half," "quarter" via folding); Indian currency (coins/notes) introduced; days/months/calendar reading; object/picture sorting for data (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §2, Grade 2 column).
**Money enters here for the first time** — informally, via NCERT's textbook scope, not yet in the NIPUN Lakshya table. This matters for the repo comparison: SRS.md's own JSON schema (§12.1) lists Money as a first-class topic, so it isn't an invented category — but its *entry point* is Class 2 (informal currency recognition), not wherever the repo currently introduces it.

## Stage 6 — Class 3 (Age 8–9) ★ NIPUN Bharat's MPL Gate

**This is the single most important checkpoint in the entire ladder.** `FLN_foundation.md` §4 flags Class III as "★ (MPL gate)" — NIPUN Bharat's 2026–27 mission deadline is explicitly defined as "every child reaches desired competencies... by end of Grade 3." `FLN_Assessment_Framework.md` §5 independently confirms Grade 3 is where "NIPUN Lakshya = GPF MPL" (India's national target and UNESCO's Global Minimum Proficiency Level formally align only at this grade). Every concept below should be treated as higher-stakes than the equivalent concept at any other stage.

**Government target, verbatim:** *"Reads/writes numbers to 9999; solves daily problems with addition/subtraction to 999; builds tables 2–10, uses division facts; measures length/weight/capacity in standard units (m, km, g, kg, litres); relates 2D shapes to 3D shapes; reads calendar dates/days, reads clock in hours & half-hours; identifies half/one-fourth/three-fourth of a whole and of a collection; identifies, extends, and communicates rules for simple number/event/shape patterns"* (`FLN_foundation.md` §4, Class III row) — independently corroborated by NAS's own Grade 3 outcome list (`FLN_Assessment_Framework.md` §2, near-verbatim match) and `Assessment_paper_rubric.md`'s "Class 4 Baseline Test" table (which, per SRS's own baseline-tests-previous-class rule, describes Class 3's target — also a near-verbatim match). Three independent sources converging is the strongest citation confidence anywhere in this framework.
**A source disagreement worth flagging precisely (not the same shape as Stage 5's):** `numeracy_research_synthesis` §1.4 cites the *2017 NCERT Learning Outcomes document*, which caps Grade 3 numbers at "up to 999," while §2's topic map (drawn from actual Math Magic textbooks) and `FLN_foundation.md`'s NIPUN Bharat Lakshya (2021) both say "up to 9999." This is **not** the synthesis document contradicting itself — it's two genuine official sources, published four years apart, giving different Grade 3 targets: the older 2017 NCERT LO document and the newer 2021 NIPUN Bharat Mission. NIPUN Bharat deliberately set numeracy targets that don't always coincide with the earlier LO document it partly supersedes. Resolution: 9999 governs, because NIPUN is the newer statutory FLN target and is independently corroborated by NAS's own Grade 3 outcome list (`FLN_Assessment_Framework.md` §2) — not merely because it "won" a 3-vs-1 count.
**Empirical reality check (why this stage needs one, uniquely):** ASER's own published assessment ladder (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §6.3) treats "2-digit subtraction with borrowing" as *the* Grade 2 benchmark — and its 2024 national data shows only 33.7% of rural Grade 3 children can do it (up from 25.9% in 2022) (`numeracy_research_synthesis` §8.1; independently confirmed by `National_status_report.md` §4: 27.6% govt-school Std III subtraction). This framework's Class 3 concepts below assume the *curricular* target (9999, standard units, formal fractions) — but any assessment design at this stage should expect a majority of students testing below it, and the level ladder needs to reach backward far enough (into Stage 4–5 concepts) to actually place them, not just forward.

## Summary — What This Framework Establishes (for the comparison phase)

1. **7 stages, not 59 levels** — concepts are decomposed by cognitive dependency, not pre-committed to a level count. Some stage/strand cells above will map to exactly one repo level; some will map to several (a repo level split across sub-skills this framework treats as one concept); some repo levels may not map to any cell here (either genuinely unfounded, or grounded in a source this framework didn't have access to and should double-check before concluding "unfounded").
2. **Every cell is cited to a specific source and section** — where two sources disagreed (Class 2 and Class 3 numeric ranges), the disagreement is stated explicitly rather than silently resolved, so the comparison phase can decide rather than inherit a hidden assumption.
3. **The Preschool 1/2/3 split is a research-*informed* construction, not a research-*specified* one** — only the Balvatika (age 5–6) endpoint is government-stated; Preschool 1–2 are backward-built from Clements & Sarama's counting trajectory. This should be checked against whatever justification (if any) the repo's own Preschool 1/2/3 split relies on.
4. **Class 4 rests on materially thinner sourcing than Preschool 3–Class 3** — it sits outside NIPUN Bharat's statutory mandate entirely. Any repo content at Class 4 that looks confident should be checked harder, not less, given the thinner ground it's standing on here.
5. **Design principles DP1–DP10** (top of this document) are the actual test to run each repo level against during comparison — not just "does the topic exist in Research/," but "does this level's *sequencing*, *CRA stage*, and *targeted misconception* match what the concept decomposition above says should be true at this stage."
6. **This document is not a fixed target — it's revised whenever new research or comparison work warrants it.** The node count above (originally 85) grew after the 2026-07-19 repo comparison surfaced a genuine missing sub-strand (shape composition) and four uncited-but-standard Class-4 topics; see the appendix's Round 4 entry and `fln_framework_evolution_log.md` for why. Expect this number to keep changing.

## Appendix: How This Document Was Built

This framework went through three rounds of external review before reaching the version above. The body of this document states only the final, settled position — this appendix is the drafting history: what was wrong in earlier drafts, how each issue was caught, and what changed. Keeping this separate from the main text is deliberate — a reader using the framework shouldn't have to wade through "this used to say X" to find out what it says now.

### Round 1 — first full review of the initial draft

**Missing concepts (the most consequential finding of this round).** The first draft's 77 concepts had real gaps the research grounds but the draft had skipped:
- **Zero** was entirely absent — no node anywhere introduced it as a number or as a place-value placeholder, even though the Stage 6 place-value misconception ("does 304's zero mean 'no tens'?") assumed a prior node that didn't exist. Fixed by adding zero-as-"none" (Stage 4) and zero-as-placeholder (Stage 5).
- **Subitizing** (instantly recognizing small quantities without counting) was missing, despite being one of Clements & Sarama's own trajectories — the same source used for the counting chain. Fixed by adding perceptual subitizing (Stage 1) and conceptual subitizing (Stage 2).
- **The number line** was missing as a concept, despite DP7 citing Siegler & Booth (2004) — a paper specifically about number-line reasoning — for an unrelated point. Fixed by adding number-line nodes at Stage 4 and Stage 5.
- **Ordinal numbers** (first, second, third) were missing entirely, despite being standard NCERT Class 1–2 content and cognitively distinct from cardinal counting.
- **Skip-counting by 2s and 5s** was missing as a bridge into the 2× and 5× multiplication tables, which otherwise had no predecessor of their own kind.
- **Area** wasn't flagged alongside the already-noted Perimeter gap, even though NCERT's Class 4 textbook covers both in the same chapters.
- **Weight** has a 4-stage hole (comparative vocabulary at Stage 2, then nothing until standard units at Stage 6) that Length and Capacity don't have — inherited honestly from the Lakshya itself, but not flagged as clearly as the Perimeter gap was. Fixed by adding an explicit flagged-gap note.

Adding these moved the total node count from 77 to 85 — a real change from finding real gaps, not scope creep.

**Two internal-consistency problems in the design principles:**
- **DP1** ("comparison precedes counting") was contradicted by the framework's own Stage 1, which has rote counting *before* comparison is introduced at Stage 2. Fixed by restating DP1 to explicitly exempt rote verbal counting (a memorized sequence, not counting in Piaget's sense) from the ordering.
- **Piaget, 1941 and Piaget, 1952** were cited in the same table row as if they were two independent supporting studies. They're the same work — the French original (*La genèse du nombre chez l'enfant*) and its English translation (*The Child's Conception of Number*). Fixed by citing it as one source.

**A theoretical tension left unflagged:** DP1 leans on Piaget's stage account, while Clements & Sarama — this framework's other main source — built their Learning Trajectories partly *in opposition to* strict Piagetian readiness constraints. Presenting both without noting the tension would have been exactly the kind of uncited confidence this framework is trying not to repeat from the repo's own level files. Fixed by adding an explicit caveat: DP1 is retained as a practical sequencing heuristic, not an uncontested theoretical claim.

**A citation given more authority than it had earned:** DP8's 50/35/15 easy/moderate/hard question-mix was presented in the same table, with the same citation weight, as Sweller, Fuson, and Rittle-Johnson — all peer-reviewed. Checking `Child_psychology.md` §8.2 directly showed it gives no primary source for this specific ratio. Fixed by relabeling it explicitly as a platform convention, not an established research standard.

**A source disagreement mischaracterized as a self-contradiction:** Stage 6 originally described the "999 vs 9999" numbers-to figure for Class 3 as an inconsistency *inside* `numeracy_research_synthesis` itself. It isn't — it's two genuine official sources (the 2017 NCERT Learning Outcomes document and the 2021 NIPUN Bharat Lakshya) published four years apart, giving different targets on purpose. Fixed by reframing it as a real disagreement between two named sources, with NIPUN Bharat governing because it's the newer statutory target and independently corroborated by NAS.

### Round 2 — verifying the round-1 fixes and checking citations against primary sources

All of round 1's fixes held up under a second, independent check. Two further issues were found:

- **A citation mix-up between two related NCF documents.** An earlier version cited "NCF 2023" for the Foundational Stage's 3-preschool-year structure. That structure is actually defined in **NCF-FS 2022** (National Curriculum Framework for the Foundational Stage, published October 2022) — a distinct, earlier document from NCF-SE 2023 (National Curriculum Framework for School Education), which restates the same structure a year later but isn't where it originates. Fixed by citing the correct document for each claim.
- **An overclaimed verification — the one real integrity problem found anywhere in this process.** A cell describing Class 4 multiplication scope stated that "tables to 15" had been "independently confirmed" against the NCERT Learning Outcomes document. That verification did not actually happen with that rigor — it was asserted, not performed. What genuinely was checked and corroborated (via web search) was that 2-digit-by-2-digit multiplication is part of the real Class 4 scope. "Tables to 15" is a widely-used convention in Indian classrooms, plausibly a reasonable platform target, but it was never actually traced to a specific, citable NCERT LO code or page. Fixed by retracting the false verification claim and re-labeling "tables to 15" honestly as unverified conventional classroom scope — the same treatment already given to other floor-vs-scope disagreements in this document, rather than inventing a special exception for it.

### Round 3 — pre-finalization check

A third review confirmed every round-2 fix held, and found two smaller, cross-document issues:

- **A wording mismatch between the two companion documents.** This document described numeral recognition (Stage 3) as "decoupled from counting," while the companion level-networks document draws a direct edge from counting fluency into numeral recognition (S2.5→S3.1) — the two documents were reading as if they disagreed with each other on the same point. ### Round 4 — driven by the 2026-07-19 repo comparison, not a re-review of existing content

Unlike Rounds 1–3 (which re-checked this document's own drafts), Round 4 was triggered by comparing this framework against the actual 59-level repo (`fln_framework_evolution_log.md`), which surfaced gaps outside this document's original scope entirely — genuinely new research, not error-correction. - **Shapes & Spatial (Chain D) had no dependency-tested edges at all** — the original document said as much, flagging it as needing "a dedicated Shapes-strand cognitive-dependency study."
