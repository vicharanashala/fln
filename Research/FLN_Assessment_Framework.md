## 1. India's Three Main Measurement Instruments

| Instrument | Conducted by | Scope | Grades |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NAS** (National Achievement Survey) | PARAKH / NCERT / MoE | School-based, census sample; periodic since 2001 | 3, 5, 8, 10 |
| **FLS 2022** (Foundational Learning Study) | MoE + NCERT + UNICEF | School-based, 20 languages, ~86,000 students, 10,000 schools | Grade 3 only |
| **ASER** (Annual Status of Education Report) | Pratham (NGO) | Rural household survey, all districts | Age 3–16 |

- NAS 2017: ~1,10,000 schools, 2,20,00,000 students. NAS 2021: ~1,18,274 schools, 5,26,824 teachers, 34,01,158 students.
- ASER 2024: 6,49,491 children, 17,997 villages, 605 rural districts; first year it also measured digital literacy (age 14–16).

## 2. NAS — Grade 3 Learning Outcomes (Numeracy)

1. Counts, reads, writes, compares numbers up to 9999
2. Solves daily problems using addition/subtraction/2+ operations, numbers up to 999
3. Constructs & uses multiplication tables 2–10, uses division facts
4. Estimates/measures length, weight, capacity (non-standard & standard units); reads calendar dates/days; reads clock in hours & half-hours
5. Identifies half/one-fourth/three-fourth of a whole and of a collection (up to 12 objects)
6. Identifies line of symmetry, congruence, compares 2D shapes; relates 2D to 3D shapes and describes properties (faces, edges, corners)
7. Identifies, extends, communicates rules for simple patterns (numbers/events/shapes) — e.g. skip count by 10
8. Solves problems with data displays (tally charts, bar graphs, pictographs), single-unit scale, up to 4 data categories

## 3. FLS 2022 — Assessment Framework (subtask design)

**Foundational Numeracy (FN) Subtasks:**

| Subtask | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Number identification | Reading aloud 16 distinct numbers in a grid (up to 999) |
| Number discrimination | Comparing 10 pairs of numbers, identifying the bigger |
| Number operations (add/sub) | Completing 4 addition + 4 subtraction facts |
| Word problems (add/sub) | Solving 6 word problems |
| Number operations (mult/div) | Constructing & using 4 multiplication facts + 4 division facts |
| Measurement | 6 problems on volume/length/time, standard & non-standard units |
| Fractions | 6 problems on half/one-fourth/three-fourth, of a whole and a collection of 12 objects |
| Patterns | Identifying & extending patterns of numbers and shapes |
| Data handling | Reading a data display, answering 6 questions |

**Foundational Literacy (FL) Subtasks** (for context; platform is Math-only): Oral Language Comprehension, Phonological Awareness, Decoding Letters, Decoding Words, Decoding Non-words, Picture Matching, Oral Reading Fluency + comprehension, Writing with visual prompt.

**Sampling**: Grade 3 students, 10,000+ schools, all 36 States/UTs. Up to 12 students/school via systematic random sampling (PIN-code-seeded random start, then every nth student from class register). Assessment is **one-on-one, oral, tablet-based**, administered by a trained Field Investigator using a digital app (built by IIT Delhi); child responds verbally/by touching screen; never coached or penalized.

## 4. Global Proficiency Framework (GPF) — Levels

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| Below Partially Meets (BPM) | Lacks most basic knowledge; cannot complete basic grade-level tasks |
| Partially Meets (PM) | Limited knowledge; partial completion of basic tasks |
| Meets (M) | Sufficient knowledge; successfully completes basic grade-level tasks |
| Exceeds (E) | Superior knowledge; completes complex grade-level tasks |

**FLS 2022 National Numeracy Results:** Meets global MPL: 42% | Partially meets: 37% | Below/exceeds: 21%.
Only **Lakshadweep** met global minimum proficiency at state level. Top performers: Jharkhand (55% Meets), Daman & Diu/DNH (52%), Tripura (50%). Bihar had the highest share exceeding proficiency (18%). Girls generally score lower than boys in numeracy across most states.

## 5. Class-wise Comparison — GPF (UNESCO) vs India (NIPUN Bharat/NAS), Numeracy

| Grade | GPF Global Standard | India (NIPUN/NAS) | Alignment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Balvatika/Pre-Gr1 | Count 1–20; 1:1 correspondence; recognize numerals; patterns | Pre-number concepts, counting, concept of zero, ELPS approach | ✓ Aligned |
| Grade 1 | Count to 100; add/subtract to 20; place value; word problems | Number system, add/subtract, place value, daily-life application | ✓ Aligned |
| Grade 2 | Add/subtract 2-digit; multiply 2–5; simple fractions (½,¼); patterns | Similar coverage; fractions less explicit | ~ Partial |
| **Grade 3 ★ MPL** | 3-digit operations; standard algorithms; fractions; measurement; data reading | 3-digit operations (own way & standard algorithm); shapes/spatial; measurement; data handling | ✓ Aligned (NIPUN Lakshya = GPF MPL) |
| Grade 4 | Multiply/divide 3-digit; compare fractions; perimeter; bar charts; shape properties | NAS tests similar skills; not GPF cut-score linked | ~ Partial |
| Grade 5 ★ SDG 4.1.1(b) | Multi-step problems; unlike-denominator fractions; area/perimeter; graphs; mean/mode | NAS 2017 data only; GPF field-tested 2019-20 but not linked | ✗ Gap |

**Key findings from the comparison:**
- Strong alignment Grades 1–3 (NIPUN's mission grades) — India's national mission is well-grounded in global standards at the foundational stage.
- Partial alignment at Grade 4 — same skill domains tested, but no formal cut-score mapping to the global 4-level scale (a technical gap, not a curriculum gap).
- Critical data gap at Grade 5 (the SDG 4.1.1 reporting point) — India's last Grade 5 data is from 2017 and isn't GPF-linked; India has been listed "data unavailable" in the UNESCO-UIS global database for 4+ years.
- India does not participate in any GPF-linked international large-scale assessment (like PIRLS/PASEC); its own National Learning Assessment (NLA, run by PARAKH) is used by the World Bank only as a best-available proxy, with limited global comparability.

## 6. Other Global Assessment Frameworks

**EGMA (Early Grade Mathematics Assessment)**
- Developed by RTI International + USAID, 2009; used in 60+ countries.
- Purpose: directly measure whether Grade 1–3 children understand basic mathematics.
- Method: one-on-one oral assessment — verbal or pointing responses, no writing required.

**UNICEF — Foundational Learning Skills (FLS) Module**
- Implemented via household surveys (e.g., MICS).
- Assessment matrix: reading accuracy, reading comprehension, inferential comprehension, number identification, number comparison, addition, pattern recognition.
- Evaluation metric: whether children achieve expected FLN skills by end of early primary; uses the concept of **Learning Poverty** (unable to read/understand simple text by age 10).
- Age group: mainly 7–14 years.

**TIMSS (Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study)**
- Run by IEA; first held 1995; every 4 years (last: 2023).
- Grades 4 (~age 10) and 8 (~age 14); Math + Science; 64 countries + 6 benchmarking systems; 650,000+ students (2023); fully digital since 2023.
- Reports on SDG 4.1.1(b) and (c) via GPF linking.

| Benchmark | Score | What students can do |
|---|---|---|
| Advanced | 625 | Apply understanding in complex/unfamiliar situations; reason, generalize, multi-step problems |
| High | 550 | Apply mathematical knowledge in a variety of situations |
| Intermediate | 475 | Apply basic knowledge in straightforward situations; whole numbers, fractions |
| Low | 400 | Basic knowledge — add/subtract up to 3-digit numbers; basic geometric properties |

Note: "Low" (400) is the baseline, **not** the MPL. GPF "Meets MPL" (Level 3) roughly corresponds to TIMSS **Intermediate** (475).

## 7. SDG 4 (Quality Education)

- UN Sustainable Development Goal 4, adopted 2015 by 193 member states (incl. India), part of the 2030 Agenda.
- India-aligned programs: NIPUN Bharat, NEP 2020, Samagra Shiksha, PARAKH.
- Current gaps: 56% of children at end of primary are "Learning Poor" (reading); India has not reported SDG 4.1.1 data in 4+ years; national assessments not formally linked to the GPF scale.

## 9. FLS 2022 vs ASER — why FLS matters more for granular design

FLS 2022 is more comprehensive than ASER for design purposes: it covers 20 languages, uses the GPF's 4-band scale, and breaks numeracy into 9 discrete subtasks (§3 above) rather than ASER's single subtraction/division benchmark. This subtask granularity is far closer to what a question-bank/competency-tagging system needs.

## 10. Relevance to Our Project

- The **FLS 2022 numeracy subtask list** (number identification, discrimination, operations, word problems, measurement, fractions, patterns, data handling) is a ready-made **competency taxonomy** — each subtask can become a "competency tag" in our Worksheet JSON schema (§10–12 of SRS), directly usable for classifying questions and mapping student errors to specific weak sub-skills.
- The **GPF 4-band scale** (BPM/PM/Meets/Exceeds) is a strong candidate model for our **AI evaluation engine's classification output** (§9 of SRS) — instead of a single pass/fail score, students could be classified into these four bands per competency, which also maps cleanly onto "next-level recommendation."
- The **NAS Grade 3 outcome list** (Section 2) gives a checked, government-approved competency list to validate our own Class-2–4 curriculum markdown files against, reducing curriculum-design risk.
- India's **Grade 4–5 GPF-linkage gap** (Section 5) is a useful point for a project report's "problem statement/motivation" section — it frames our platform (which produces continuously updated, competency-linked, individual student data) as directly addressing a data gap that even national assessments currently can't close.
- The **one-on-one, oral, tablet-based** administration model used by FLS is architecturally similar to what our ICR/scanning + Python evaluation pipeline is trying to digitize at scale for paper-based exams (§8–9 of SRS) — useful precedent to cite when justifying our ICR approach as methodologically sound.
