## 1. Headline National Metrics (2024)

- **6,49,491** children surveyed, across **605** rural districts, **17,997** villages (ASER 2024)
- ~50% of children in India lack foundational learning (World Bank)
- 36 States/UTs have a NIPUN Bharat Steering Committee
- 25 lakh+ teachers trained under NIPUN
- Std III reading at grade level: **23.4%** (up from 16.3% in 2022)
- Std III subtraction competency: **33.7%** (all schools) / 27.6% (govt. schools only)
- Budget allocation FY 2024-25: **Rs 73,498 crore** (highest ever for school education)

## 2. Enrollment & Access

**Pre-primary enrollment:**
| Age | 2018 | 2022 | 2024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3-year-olds | 68.1% | 75.8% | 77.4% |
| 4-year-olds | 76.0% | 82.0% | 83.4% |
| 5-year-olds | 58.4% | 62.1% | 71.4% |

- States near-universal (>95%) pre-primary enrollment (age 4): Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Odisha.
- Lagging (>50% of 3-year-olds unenrolled): Meghalaya, Uttar Pradesh.
- 'Underage' children in Std I dropped from 25.6% (2018) to **16.7% (2024)** — lowest ever recorded.

**Elementary enrollment (age 6–14):** 98.1% overall (2024); Govt schools 66.8%, Private ~29% (partial post-pandemic recovery from the 2022 peak in govt-school enrollment).

**School infrastructure/attendance (govt. primary schools):**
| Indicator | 2018 | 2022 | 2024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Avg. student attendance | 72.4% | 73.0% | 75.9% |
| Avg. teacher attendance | 85.1% | 86.8% | 87.5% |
| Usable girls' toilets | 66.4% | 68.4% | 72.0% |
| Drinking water | 74.8% | 76.1% | 77.7% |
| Books beyond textbooks | 36.9% | 43.9% | 51.3% |

Over 52% of govt. primary schools now have fewer than 60 students enrolled ("small schools" are the new norm); over two-thirds of Std I–II classrooms are multigrade.

## 3. Reading Outcomes (ASER 2024)

**Std III (% reading Std II-level text):**
| | 2018 | 2022 | 2024 (Govt) | 2024 (Pvt) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| All India | 20.9% | 16.3% | 23.4% | ~43% |

- Highest gainers (govt., 2022→2024, >10pp): Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Kerala, UP, Haryana, Odisha, Maharashtra.
- Despite recovery, **fewer than 1 in 4** govt-school Std III children read at grade level.

**Std V:** National (govt.) 44.8% (2024), up from 38.5% (2022). Top: Himachal Pradesh (70.1%), Punjab (66.0%), Mizoram (65.9%). Persistent laggards: J&K (21.8%), Assam (32.8%), Nagaland (27.1%).

**Std VIII:** National (govt.) 67.5% (2024). Andhra Pradesh and Telangana show a multi-year **declining** trend (AP: 78.6%→53.0%; Telangana: 63.1%→50.8%, 2018→2024).

## 4. Arithmetic Outcomes (ASER 2024)

**Std III (% doing subtraction):**
| | 2018 | 2022 | 2024 |
|---|---|---|---|
| All India (Govt) | 20.9% | 20.2% | 27.6% |
| All India (Overall) | 28.2% | 25.9% | 33.7% |

Top: Mizoram (55.3%), Himachal Pradesh (49.5%), Punjab (45.8%). Weakest: Rajasthan (10.4%), Madhya Pradesh (13.0%). Gujarat shows an anomalous **decline** (22.8%→16.5%) despite strong pre-primary enrollment.

**Std V (% doing division):** All India 30.7% (2024), up from 25.6% (2022). Top: Punjab (50.0%), Himachal Pradesh (47.0%). Weak: Assam (12.0%), Rajasthan (12.3%).

**Std VIII (% doing division):** All India 45.8% (2024) — nearly flat since 2018 (44.1%), a concern since most FLN interventions stop by Grade 8. Kerala shows a steady **decline** in this metric (43.3%→31.0%) despite strong reading scores.

| Know how to use a smartphone | 85.5% | 79.4% | 82.2% |

- Over 75% of children completed all 3 digital tasks tested (setting an alarm, browsing, finding/sharing a YouTube video).

- Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala: girls outperform or match boys on digital tasks.

## 7. Structural Challenges

- **Language**: 22 official languages, 19,500+ mother tongues; India has lost 220+ languages in 50 years, 197 classified endangered (UNESCO); 25% of primary children face moderate-to-severe disadvantage from classroom/home language mismatch (World Bank 2021).
- **COVID-19 learning loss**: ~250 million Indian students affected by closures; 92% lost at least one language ability, 82% lost at least one math ability (UNICEF 2022); global 10-year-olds unable to read simple text rose from 57%→70% (2019–2022); global economic cost estimated at $17 trillion in lifetime earnings (14% of global GDP).
- **Teacher quality**: >80% of schools received FLN in-person training, but quality/follow-through varies; many teachers lack metalinguistic understanding for phonics/decoding instruction; two-thirds of Std I–II classrooms are multigrade.
- **Assessment quality**: NAS/FLS don't yet give **annual, grade-level, multi-skill** FLN tracking at the scale needed for real-time correction.
- **Equity**: girls score lower in numeracy (not reading); private-school students consistently outperform govt-school peers at all grades; North-east/BIMARU states lag in both literacy and numeracy; 7.9% of girls aged 15–16 are out of school (>10% in MP, UP, Rajasthan, Mizoram, Gujarat).

## 8. National Summary Scorecard (selected)

| Indicator | Latest Value | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Overall enrollment (6–14) | 98.1% (2024) | Achieved |
| Std III reading (Govt) | 23.4% (2024) | Critical gap |
| Std III subtraction (All) | 33.7% (2024) | Critical gap |
| Std V division (All) | 30.7% (2024) | Critical gap |
| Grade 3 literacy meets GPF (FLS 2022) | 45% | Critical gap |
| Grade 3 numeracy meets GPF (FLS 2022) | 42% | Critical gap |
| FLN directives to schools | >80% | On track |
| Teacher FLN training coverage | >80% | On track |

**Overall assessment**: India has made measurable progress on FLN inputs/enrollment, but with fewer than 1 in 4 Std III govt-school children reading at grade level, and NIPUN Bharat's 2026–27 deadline close, a substantial gap between ambition and achievement persists. Language-medium instruction, teacher quality, and equity for girls/marginalized groups remain the central bottlenecks.

## 9. EAC-PM Report — FLN Index Structure

- 36 indicators across 5 domains: **Educational Infrastructure, Access to Education, Basic Health, Learning Outcomes, Governance**.
- Built using Principal Component Analysis; used to rank states/UTs against each other.

## 10. Relevance to Our Project

- The **Literacy-Numeracy gap** (Grade 5: 30.7% can do division vs 48.7%/44.8% read fluently — an ~18pp gap, ASER 2024) is direct quantitative justification for building a **dedicated Math-FLN platform** (as scoped in §1.4 of the SRS) rather than a combined literacy+numeracy tool — numeracy is the demonstrably weaker, more neglected domain.
- These national baseline numbers (Std III subtraction: 27.6% govt; Std V division: 30.7%) are the exact figures to cite in a project report's **"problem statement"/motivation** section, and can also serve as an external benchmark to compare against our platform's own pilot-cohort assessment results once deployed.
- The **governance/monitoring gap** (weakest EAC-PM pillar, avg. 28/100 per the earlier comparison file) supports the SRS's emphasis on a **full role-hierarchy with audit logbook and dashboards** (§13–14 of SRS) — directly targeting the governance weakness the national data identifies.
- The **teacher-training-input vs learning-outcome gap** ("input availability does not automatically translate to learning outcomes") is a strong rationale for our platform's **closed feedback loop** — AI evaluation → auto-flagging weak questions/competencies → ticketing system → curriculum revision (§1.4, §9 of SRS) — since it directly targets the disconnect between training inputs and actual outcomes that ASER/EAC-PM data repeatedly surfaces.
- The **language-mismatch finding** (25% of children disadvantaged) is a reminder to keep in view even though the current SRS scope is Math-only and English/Hindi medium — a useful note for the "Out of Scope/Future Versions" discussion around potential multi-language worksheet support (already listed in SRS §19).
