# **Sub-Level 53.2** 

### **Description**

Students build foundational understanding of factors and multiples using multiplication tables and simple number patterns.

 Activities focus on recognizing multiplication relationships before listing factors and multiples independently.

## **Sections**

### **Section A: Circle the Factors**

Examples

**Factor of 18**

Options:

2 3 5 6 9

### **Section B: Complete the Multiples**

Examples

3, 6, 9, \_\_, \_\_

8, 16, 24, \_\_

### **Section C: Factor Pairs**

Examples

12 \= □ × □

20 \= □ × □

### **Section D: Match**

Match multiplication facts to the correct number.

Example:

3 × 4 → 12

5 × 6 → 30
